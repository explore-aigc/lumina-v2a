Abstract

Audio-video synchronization is a crucial challenge for high-fidelity video-to-audio (V2A) generation. However, existing methods often struggle to produce audio that is both semantically meaningful and temporally synchronized with silent video. To address this challenge, we propose a Lumina-V2A, an efficient flow-based model that jointly leverages the video and text to guide audio generation. Our framework features two key components, including a multimodal conditioning module for introducing semantic and frame-level temporal guidance, and a co-attention-based DiT block for efficiently interacting aligned video-audio embeddings with conditions. To further improve video-audio synchronization using feedback from preference data, we pioneeringly propose a Semantic-Temporal Alignment-Ranked Direct Preference Optimization (STAR-DPO), a novel automatic framework that iteratively synthesizes preference audio-video pairs and optimizes them to enhance V2A alignment at the content and temporal level. Our extensive experiments on the VGGSound benchmark demonstrate that Lumina-V2A achieves the state-of-the-art performance across various evaluation metrics using the fewest trainable parameters.
